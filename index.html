Analysis of Selective and Indiscriminate Violence in Colombia

Figure 1 illustrates the experimental design developed to explore hypotheses regarding armed conflict in Colombia, utilizing official data spanning from 1958 to 2021.

Data concerning collective violence (both cases and victims) are structured into monthly time series. This is done for each distinct dimension of violence: Selective Violence (VS), Indiscriminate Violence (VI), and Collective Violence (VC), which is the sum of VS and VI. This process yields a total of six time series (Cases VS, Cases VI, Victims VS, Victims VI, Cases VC, and Victims VC).

Each of these six time series undergoes normalization to represent temporal dynamics. Escalation is defined with values +1, 0, or -1, indicating whether the case/victim count in month $t$ is greater than, equal to, or less than the count in month $t-1$, respectively. Intensity is also defined with values +1, 0, or -1, indicating whether the count in month $t$ is greater than, equal to, or less than the average ($\mu$) count over the preceding three years (36 months).

For forecasting future violence dynamics, three distinct models employing different sliding windows are proposed. These models aim to determine the highest probability of the state (+1, 0, or -1 for Escalation and Intensity) occurring in month $t+1$. This prediction is based on the observed combination of Escalation and Intensity states in month $t-1$. Specifically, the models estimate the probability of a state at $t+1$ by comparing the state at $t-1$ to similar states observed at various points ($t_n$) within a historical reference period.

The forecasting performance for Escalation and Intensity will be evaluated both separately and in a scenario that combines the two metrics. The results produced by the three proposed probability models will be compared against the performance of a baseline weighted moving average model, also utilizing an n-sized sliding window. Model evaluation will primarily use the weighted F1 score metric, contrasting the performance with a traditional weighted moving average approach as described by Perry (2010).

This project focuses on the quantitative analysis of Selective Violence (VS) and Indiscriminate Violence (VI) in Colombia. Using detailed data on violent events (Cases) and affected persons (Victims), we aim to understand the dynamics of violence at the national, departmental, and regional levels.

The analysis includes data cleaning and processing, and the determination of key metrics such as **Escalation** (comparison with the previous month) and **Intensity** (comparison with the weighted average of the last 3 years) for both Cases and Victims.

About the Project

This repository contains the source code and scripts used for the analysis of Selective Violence (VS) and Indiscriminate Violence (VI) in Colombia. The objective is to provide tools for data processing and metric calculation that allow for monitoring and understanding the evolution of these types of violence across different geographical levels of the country.

Key Metrics

The project calculates the following metrics for Cases and Victims at the Country, Department, and Region levels:

  • **Escalation:** Change in the number of Cases or Victims compared to the immediately preceding month.
  • **Intensity:** Comparison of the current number of Cases or Victims with the weighted average over the last 3 years.

Exact formulas are detailed in the README.md.

Results

Results from data processing and metric calculation are stored in the results/ folder. These include summary tables and potentially visualizations generated by the scripts or notebooks.
